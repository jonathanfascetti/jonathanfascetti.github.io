Housing Options

Towards the end of oneâ€™s sentence, an option to start reintegrating into society called transitional housing may become available. In this type of environment, the soon-to-be-free prisoners learn the essential life skills for when they return to society.

Those who stay in transitional housing do not stay for a long time as they are expected to organize their own housing during their time there. Sometimes, staying in a transitional house is mandatory. Depending on the housing, being homeless is sometimes a prerequisite for using the housing to prioritize those who need it most.

Post release, there are services that help ex-cons stay afloat while they work to get back on their feet. For example, halfway houses help people recover themselves by giving them a bed to sleep in, etc.

Campaign

Ban the Box

The Ban the Box campaign is an organization with the goal of eliminating the stigma for ex-cons that is structured into society. On many job applications, housing contracts, school applications, and more, there is a required box that must be checked stating that the applicant has never been convicted of a crime. For those without a clean record (even as small as a misdemeanor), this box can act as a severe obstacle for these foundational needs.

This organization has done some great work and has successfully encouraged some states and cities to remove this requirement which has allowed more people to secure jobs despite their criminal record.
