Reform is needed

Reform on Housing

Equal Opportunity

The primary goal that housing reform should have is focused around getting more free beds available to ex-convicts.

As of right now, there are a lot of different types of housing that have different levels of prerequisites to attend (including cost). To aid the crisis of many homeless ex-cons, much of this housing should be converted into free beds.

This conversion of private housing to public beds would have a chain reaction in how the ex-cons can reintegrate into society. By having geographically various housing options (being sure to avoid designated drug-free zones to accommodate for ex-cons with drug related charges), more ex-cons would be exposed to areas out of high-risk ones, thus reducing the risk of exhibiting recidivism. Inversly, it would expose the general public to coexisting with ex-cons, dampening the stigma against them.

Housing Reform

Geographical Change

A lot of the change that needs to happen revolved around the housing locations' geography. The image directly to the left is of housing locations from the organization MassHousing. It is important to note that there are more services that have more housing options, but these are just from MassHousing. Compared to the image below, which represents population density of Massachussetts, there is not equitable alignment, especially in wealthier suburbs and in the south end area between Foxborough and New Bedford as well as along the Cape.

A change to a more even distribution of housiung services thoughout the state would allow those living in the houses exposure to more diverse communities, but it would also expose people living in the communities to ex-cons. This sort of subjection for those in the general public may help lessen the stigma that some hold against those who have been formally incarcerated, despite their sentence already having been served.

Integration of education in the new housing system would also be necessary. Those who have received a high school diploma (or higher) are significantly less likely to be arrested. Implementing services within the housing program or providing access to community colleges would give the ex-cons utilizing the services an advantage when it comes to their ability to reintegrate with society.

Funding

Funding for this new housing program could come either from Community Development Block Grants, emergency shelter grant programs, or federal tax credit programs.

Changes should be made on a national scale, but trials should be run on a local level first.
