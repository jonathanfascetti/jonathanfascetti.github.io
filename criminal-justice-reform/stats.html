Essential Statistics

Understanding the challenge

In recent years, the number of people in jail has increased dramatically. With that, many of those prisoners will be released back into general society over time. It is incredibly hard for ex-cons to successfully reintegrate into society post-release. There is also a harsh stigma against those released from prison which can make it difficult for them to secure housing, a job, or public assistance (food stamps, addiction treatment, etc.).

A report from the US Sentencing Commissions has shown that 50% of those released from prisons are rearrested within 8 years. This shockingly high statistic is arguably mostly due to their inability to reintegrate into society.

Better programs to help recently released prisoners reintegrate into society are necessary.

Housing

Many of those released from prisons (especially if their situation was unstable to begin with) do not necesarily have a home to go to once they are released into society. There are shelters that homeless people can go to, but running out of beds is a big issue. According to McKernan, Patricia; 2017, 23% of those in sheltered homes identify with having been incarcerated in the past two years. Similarly, 10% of those incarcerated are homeless to begin with. Reform as it relates to housing is needed to reduce the number of convicts in high risk situations.

Education

It has been seen through research that having as much as a high school diploma can significantly lower one's chances of being arrested. Because of this, it will be neceassry to increase the amount of access to educational services available in transitional houses.
