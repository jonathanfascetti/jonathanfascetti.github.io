What is DNA?

DeoxyriboNucleic Acid

In the human body, we are made up of trillions of individual cells. And within each of those cells, strands of DNA are stored in 23 packages, called chromosomes. The DNA acts like a code to control how the cell (and the whole body) functions. DNA, which is a chemical compound, stands for deoxyribonucleic acid, but you may know it as a long series of A’s, C’s, G’s, and T’s. Each of the four letters stands for a type of nucleotide molecule: A is adenine, C is cytosine, G is guanine, and T is thymine (see image). Together, they act as instructions that our bodies read to perform functions by making protein (learn more about protein synthesis here).

DNA is in all living things, including the microorganism that make up microbiomes. Scientists can extract the genetic code, and sequence it to determine what microbes are in the sample.
